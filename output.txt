Hint: Most people with second-degree Mobitz type 1 heart block won't experience any symptoms.
Symptom(s): any symptoms
----------------------------------------------
Hint: However, some people may have symptoms such as:
Symptom(s):   light-headedness
 dizziness 
 fainting (temporary loss of consciousness) 
 
----------------------------------------------
Hint: They may also have additional symptoms such as:
Symptom(s):   chest pain, which may be worse during physical activity, such as climbing the stairs
 shortness of breath  
 tiring easily when doing physical activity 
 feeling very dizzy suddenly when standing up from a lying or sitting position; this is caused by having low blood pressure (hypotension) 
 
----------------------------------------------
Hint: Symptoms of congenital third-degree heart block in older infants or young children include:
Symptom(s):   unusually pale and blotchy skin
 lethargy (a lack of energy) 
 unwillingness to take part in exercise or physical activity 
 dizziness 
 fainting 
 
----------------------------------------------
Hint: Symptoms of acquired third-degree heart block include:
Symptom(s):   light-headedness
 dizziness 
 fainting 
 fatigue (extreme tiredness) 
 chest pain 
 slow heart beat (bradycardia) 
 
----------------------------------------------

Hint: The most common symptoms of coronary heart disease (CHD) are chest pain (angina) and a heart attack.
Symptom(s): chest pain
( angina )
a heart attack

----------------------------------------------
Hint: You can also experience other symptoms, such as heart palpitations and unusual breathlessness.
Symptom(s): such as heart palpitations and unusual breathlessness
----------------------------------------------
Hint: During a heart attack, you may also experience the following symptoms:
Symptom(s):   pain in other parts of the body - it can feel as if the pain is travelling from your chest to your arms, jaw, neck, back and abdomen
 lightheadedness 
 sweating 
 nausea 
 breathlessness 
 
----------------------------------------------

Symptom(s): hacking cough

Symptom(s):   sore throat
 headache 
 runny or blocked nose 
 aches and pains 
 tiredness 
 
----------------------------------------------

Symptom(s): shortness of breath or wheezing , due to inflamed airways

http://www.nhs.uk/Conditions/Depression/Pages/Symptoms.aspx
Hint: The psychological symptoms of depression include:
Symptom(s):   continuous low mood or sadness
 feeling hopeless and helpless 
 having low self-esteem  
 feeling tearful 
 feeling guilt-ridden 
 feeling irritable and intolerant of others  
 having no motivation or interest in things 
 finding it difficult to make decisions 
 not getting any enjoyment out of life 
 feeling anxious or worried  
 having suicidal thoughts or thoughts of harming yourself 
 
----------------------------------------------
Hint: The physical symptoms of depression include:
Symptom(s):   moving or speaking more slowly than usual
 changes in appetite or weight (usually decreased, but sometimes increased)  
 constipation  
 unexplained aches and pains 
 lack of energy 
 low sex drive (loss of libido) 
 changes to your menstrual cycle 
 disturbed sleep – for example, finding it difficult to fall asleep at night or waking up very early in the morning 
 
----------------------------------------------
Hint: The social symptoms of depression include:
Symptom(s):   not doing well at work
 avoiding contact with friends and taking part in fewer social activities 
 neglecting your hobbies and interests 
 having difficulties in your home and family life 
 
----------------------------------------------
